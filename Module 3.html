Security and Risk Management

Welcome to the Security and Risk Management Module. Security and risk are increasingly being treated as inseparable as enterprises move away from traditional siloed views of business towards more integrated, enterprise solutions. In this module we will look at the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. We will review the various methods of risk assessment â€“ looking at both qualitative and quantitative methods. We will discuss a number of more traditional risk models (such as STRIDE, DREAD and Attack Trees) as well as reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We will also review common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. Finally, we will discuss some of the key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management.

Reflections

What exactly have I learnt and how?

Qualitative and quantitative risk assessments:
Learning qualitative and quantitative risk assessments involves understanding the concepts, methodologies, and techniques used to assess and manage risks in various contexts.
1. Understand the Basics: Familiarize yourself with the fundamental concepts of risk, including the difference between qualitative and quantitative risk assessments. Learn about the risk management process and its different stages, such as risk identification, analysis, evaluation, treatment, and monitoring.
2. Study Risk Assessment Methods: For qualitative risk assessment, explore techniques like Risk Probability and Impact Assessment, Risk Matrix, Risk Scoring, and Risk Categorization. Understand how to assess and prioritize risks based on their likelihood and potential impact on the project or organization. For quantitative risk assessment, study methods like Monte Carlo simulation, sensitivity analysis, expected monetary value (EMV), and decision trees. These techniques involve assigning numerical values to risk probabilities and impacts for more precise analysis.
3. Learn Risk Assessment Standards and Frameworks: Familiarize yourself with international risk assessment standards and frameworks, such as ISO 31000, COSO ERM, and NIST SP 800-30. These documents provide guidelines for effective risk management practices.
4. Practice with Case Studies: Work on practical case studies or real-world scenarios to apply both qualitative and quantitative risk assessment techniques. This hands-on experience will help you understand how to identify, assess, and respond to different types of risks.
